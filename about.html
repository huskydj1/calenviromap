Mission

As the consumer demand for plastic-made products increases, millions of tons of plastic enter the oceans every year. The resulting plastic pollution kills marine animals by ingestion and entanglement, and these particles also break down to release harmful chemicals such as phthalates and BPA which can affect peopleâ€™s reproduction and development (1). Today, this marine debris has already accumulated into garbage patches three times the size of France (2). By 2050, there may be more plastic in our oceans than fish, threatening the entire food chain (3).

There is still time to combat this global catastrophe. While organizations such as The Ocean Cleanup clean up the floating plastics directly, others such as Heal the Bay have been fighting for the environment through education, research, and lobbying. All of these tasks are aided by data, allowing measures to specifically target the communities that need them the most.

Ctrash aims to make it easier for anyone to contribute to this effort. Using computer vision technologies, this website identifies and categorizes trash in images, making it easier to collect data. You may view these reports from all around the world on our interactive map. Further, our database is reviewed and annotated so that open-source initiatives like the TACO trash dataset may take advantage of our results. Ultimately, we create a space for anyone to help preserve the natural world for future generations.
