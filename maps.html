Maps

In a world with billions of competing voices and interests, numbers have long been held as the most objective way of determining the truth. The public debate surrounding COVID-19 exemplifies data’s perceived value, with every major newspaper broadcasting the number of positive patients and moralities and with the words “flatten the curve” on everyone’s lips.

What’s been forgotten, however, is that statistics, just like any other science, is a continuous process of observation, skepticism, and testing. This means that not only is the data itself important (as CalEnviroMap’s feed wall and experiences map hope to cultivate) but also its interpretation. Without rigorous and rational discussions, there will always be ways of sampling and manipulating data to feed your bias, opening up “opportunities to create distrust, derail discussions of effective strategies and form part of deliberate disinformation campaigns.”

This page hopes to facilitate public and expert discussion on collecting and analyzing data by providing a simple format of presenting and debating one’s take. To that end, it will allow users to 1) post novel datasets and analysis of climate changes’ impact in California 2) like or dislike posts and 3) allow users to “fork” previous posts and add their own take.
